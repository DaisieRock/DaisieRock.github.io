A Blurb on my research

I work with continuous versions of historically discrete structures: representation categories, derived categories, and cluster categories. I am interested in how the homological structures of these categories are connected to cluster algebras, Lie algebras, Grassmannians, and persistent homology. In the discrete (and usually finite) world there are many connections among these notions. In the world of the continuum, how many of these connections hold or exist in a similar fashion? What can we learn about the discrete structure that was hidden by all the finiteness and discreteness assumptions?

My FWO project is about infinite-dimensional Grassmannians.

My thesis was Continuous Quivers of Type A, most of which is contained in series of four papers (arXiv link).
